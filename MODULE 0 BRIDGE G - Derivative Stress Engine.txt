MODULE 0 BRIDGE G

DERIVATIVE STRESS ENGINE

PURPOSE

Monitor derivative market conditions to detect:

* gamma pressure
* dealer hedging pressure
* volatility instability
* liquidation cascades
* convexity events
* market reflexivity

before they become visible in price.

---

CORE PRINCIPLE

Modern markets are heavily influenced by derivatives.

Price does not move only because of:

* fundamentals
* macro data
* technical analysis

Price also moves because:

* dealers hedge
* options expire
* volatility changes
* positions become unstable

Derivatives frequently amplify market moves.

---

PRIMARY QUESTIONS

1. Are dealers long gamma?

2. Are dealers short gamma?

3. Is hedging stabilizing price?

4. Is hedging destabilizing price?

5. Is volatility being suppressed?

6. Is volatility being amplified?

7. Is convexity pressure building?

8. Is convexity pressure declining?

9. Is liquidation risk increasing?

10. Is liquidation risk decreasing?

---

DERIVATIVE REGIMES

REGIME 1

POSITIVE GAMMA

Characteristics:

* dealers long gamma
* dealers buy dips
* dealers sell rallies

Implications:

* reduced volatility
* range behavior
* mean reversion

---

REGIME 2

NEUTRAL GAMMA

Characteristics:

* balanced dealer positioning

Implications:

* normal market behavior

---

REGIME 3

NEGATIVE GAMMA

Characteristics:

* dealers short gamma
* dealers chase price

Implications:

* trend acceleration
* volatility expansion

---

REGIME 4

CONVEXITY STRESS

Characteristics:

* aggressive dealer hedging
* unstable positioning

Implications:

* violent price movement
* squeeze potential

---

REGIME 5

LIQUIDATION CASCADE

Characteristics:

* forced unwinds
* rapid deleveraging
* margin stress

Implications:

* extreme volatility
* disorderly price action
