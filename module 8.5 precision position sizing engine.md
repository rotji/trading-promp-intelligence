==================================================
MODULE 8.5
PRECISION POSITION SIZING ENGINE
==================================================

PURPOSE
==================================================

The purpose of this module is determining:

* how much exposure the environment justifies
* how much leverage survivability permits
* how much fragility the structure can tolerate
* how much asymmetry quality deserves deployment
* how much volatility exposure remains survivable
* how much continuation durability justifies participation
* how much capital risk remains rational

before live exposure begins.

The objective is NOT maximizing profits.

The objective is maximizing survivable expectancy.

==================================================
CORE POSITION SIZING PRINCIPLE
==================================================

Exposure deployment must adapt according to:

* environmental quality
* execution efficiency
* asymmetry durability
* volatility stability
* liquidity persistence
* continuation durability
* structural persistence
* systemic fragility
* nonlinear-event probability

rather than emotional conviction.

The system must distinguish between:

* attractive opportunity
and
* survivable exposure.

==================================================
SURVIVABILITY PRINCIPLE
==================================================

The system must prioritize:

* survivability
* expectancy durability
* asymmetry preservation
* nonlinear fragility reduction
* long-term capital persistence

above short-term aggression.

The system must recognize that:

* oversized exposure destroys durability
* leverage amplifies volatility instability
* fragility compounds nonlinearly
* survivability matters more than temporary expansion

==================================================
POSITION SIZING CLASSIFICATION
==================================================

The system must classify exposure into:

1. AGGRESSIVE EXPOSURE
2. BALANCED EXPOSURE
3. REDUCED EXPOSURE
4. DEFENSIVE EXPOSURE
5. MINIMAL EXPOSURE
6. ZERO EXPOSURE

before participation occurs.

==================================================
1. AGGRESSIVE EXPOSURE
==================================================

Characteristics:

* elite execution quality
* elite asymmetry quality
* strong continuation durability
* controlled volatility behavior
* strong liquidity persistence
* stable systemic conditions

Implications:

* larger participation justified
* stronger leverage tolerance justified

==================================================
2. BALANCED EXPOSURE
==================================================

Characteristics:

* favorable execution quality
* manageable volatility behavior
* acceptable liquidity conditions
* balanced continuation durability
* acceptable asymmetry quality

Implications:

* standard participation justified

==================================================
3. REDUCED EXPOSURE
==================================================

Characteristics:

* mixed continuation quality
* elevated volatility sensitivity
* moderate liquidity fragility
* moderate asymmetry deterioration
* elevated adaptation requirements

Implications:

* reduced leverage preferred
* reduced participation preferred

==================================================
4. DEFENSIVE EXPOSURE
==================================================

Characteristics:

* fragile continuation quality
* elevated volatility instability
* elevated liquidity deterioration probability
* elevated nonlinear-event sensitivity
* unstable asymmetry durability

Implications:

* highly defensive participation required

==================================================
5. MINIMAL EXPOSURE
==================================================

Characteristics:

* severe instability risk
* elevated systemic fragility
* elevated continuation instability
* elevated execution uncertainty

Implications:

* speculative exposure only

==================================================
6. ZERO EXPOSURE
==================================================

Characteristics:

* severe asymmetry deterioration
* severe volatility instability
* severe liquidity deterioration
* severe systemic instability
* irrational exposure conditions

Implications:

* participation rejected entirely

==================================================
VOLATILITY-ADJUSTED SIZING PRINCIPLE
==================================================

Position sizing must adapt according to:

* volatility compression
* volatility expansion
* nonlinear expansion probability
* instability acceleration probability
* reversal sensitivity

The system must recognize that:

* stable volatility supports larger sizing
* unstable volatility requires defensive sizing
* nonlinear expansion increases fragility

==================================================
ASYMMETRY-WEIGHTED SIZING PRINCIPLE
==================================================

Exposure quality improves when:

* invalidation remains controlled
* continuation potential remains durable
* asymmetry quality remains favorable
* liquidity persistence remains stable
* expansion durability remains constructive

Exposure quality deteriorates when:

* invalidation expands excessively
* continuation quality weakens materially
* asymmetry compresses materially
* volatility instability accelerates
* liquidity persistence deteriorates

==================================================
LIQUIDITY-ADJUSTED SIZING PRINCIPLE
==================================================

Position sizing must evaluate whether:

* liquidity continuation remains constructive
* spread/slippage conditions remain efficient
* execution absorption remains healthy
* reclaim behavior remains durable
* execution friction remains manageable

Exposure must reduce when:

* liquidity instability accelerates
* spread/slippage deteriorates materially
* execution friction weakens expectancy
* volatility instability increases execution uncertainty

==================================================
NONLINEAR FRAGILITY PRINCIPLE
==================================================

The system must recognize that:

* fragility compounds nonlinearly
* leverage amplifies instability
* volatility acceleration increases liquidation risk
* systemic instability increases discontinuity probability

The system must reduce exposure aggressively when:

* nonlinear-event sensitivity rises
* continuation durability weakens materially
* volatility instability accelerates materially
* liquidity deterioration accelerates materially

==================================================
SCALING PRINCIPLE
==================================================

The system may determine:

* scaling into continuation justified
* scaling out defensively justified
* asymmetric reduction justified
* continuation expansion participation justified
* defensive exposure reduction justified

according to evolving continuation durability.

==================================================
RISK DISTRIBUTION PRINCIPLE
==================================================

The system must recognize that:

* concentration risk increases fragility
* correlated exposure compounds instability
* leverage concentration destroys survivability
* systemic stress increases nonlinear correlation risk

The system must prioritize:

* survivable distribution
* controlled exposure concentration
* adaptive capital deployment
* nonlinear fragility reduction

The system must generate:

* survivability score

* liquidity durability score
* leverage safety score
* nonlinear fragility score

before exposure deployment occurs.
