MODULE 0 BRIDGE C

INTEREST RATE SWAP ENGINE

PURPOSE

Monitor the global interest rate swap market to detect:

* rate expectations
* liquidity conditions
* duration pressure
* yield instability
* central bank transmission effects

Detect structural shifts before they fully appear in:

* bond markets
* FX markets
* equity markets
* credit markets

---

CORE PRINCIPLE

Interest Rate Swaps are the largest derivative market in existence.

Interest Rate Swaps often reveal:

* future rate expectations
* institutional positioning
* funding conditions
* duration demand

before asset prices fully react.

---

WHAT IS AN INTEREST RATE SWAP

Basic structure:

One party pays fixed interest.

One party pays floating interest.

Used for:

* hedging
* duration management
* funding management
* macro positioning

---

PRIMARY QUESTIONS

1. Is the market expecting higher rates?

2. Is the market expecting lower rates?

3. Is duration demand increasing?

4. Is duration demand decreasing?

5. Is bond market stress increasing?

6. Is bond market stress decreasing?

7. Are swap spreads widening?

8. Are swap spreads tightening?

9. Is monetary transmission functioning normally?

10. Is monetary transmission breaking down?

---

INTEREST RATE REGIMES

REGIME 1

EASING EXPECTATION

Characteristics:

* falling rate expectations
* falling yields
* supportive liquidity

Implications:

* bullish equities
* bullish bonds
* weaker defensive demand

---

REGIME 2

NEUTRAL EXPECTATION

Characteristics:

* stable yields
* stable funding conditions

Implications:

* balanced market environment

---

REGIME 3

TIGHTENING EXPECTATION

Characteristics:

* rising yields
* rising funding costs
* tighter liquidity

Implications:

* stronger defensive positioning
* pressure on risk assets

---

REGIME 4

RATE STRESS

Characteristics:

* rapid yield movement
* duration instability
* swap market stress

Implications:

* volatility expansion
* asset repricing

---

REGIME 5

SYSTEMIC RATE INSTABILITY

Characteristics:

* disorderly yield movement
* funding transmission problems
* intervention risk

Implications:

* liquidity disruption
* cross-asset instability

---

KEY INPUTS

Monitor:

* Treasury yields

* OIS markets
* SOFR markets
* Fed expectations
* ECB expectations
* BOJ expectations
* yield curve behavior

---

FX IMPACT

Interest rate swaps influence:

* carry trades
* currency valuation
* yield differentials
* capital flows

Higher expected rates:

Increase probability of:

* currency strength
* carry attractiveness

---

Lower expected rates:

Increase probability of:

* currency weakness
* carry deterioration

---

EXECUTION RULE

Always compare:

* spot market behavior
* yield behavior
* swap behavior

before entering:

* FX trades
* carry trades
* bond trades

---

WARNING SIGNALS

Increase caution if:

* yields diverge from price
* swap spreads widen aggressively
* funding costs spike
* duration pressure increases

---

FINAL PRINCIPLE

Interest Rate Swaps are one of the primary transmission systems between:

central banks

↓

bond markets

↓

currencies

↓

equities

↓

global liquidity

Monitoring swap conditions helps identify macro regime changes before they become obvious in price.
