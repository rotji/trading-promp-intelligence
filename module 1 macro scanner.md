STEP 5F — MARKET SURVIVAL & EXTINCTION ENGINE

Purpose:
Analyze the long-term survivability, adaptability, dominance potential, or extinction risk of markets, sectors, narratives, business models, asset classes, and economic structures.

Markets evolve through competitive survival dynamics.

Some structures adapt and dominate.
Others weaken, fragment, or disappear.

--------------------------------------------------

A. SURVIVAL DETECTION

Evaluate whether assets, sectors, or systems demonstrate:

- adaptability
- resilience
- innovation capacity
- liquidity durability
- narrative persistence
- institutional support
- capital attraction
- structural competitiveness
- technological relevance

--------------------------------------------------

B. EXTINCTION RISK ANALYSIS

Detect whether systems face risks such as:

- technological disruption
- liquidity deterioration
- narrative collapse
- structural obsolescence
- declining participation
- capital abandonment
- regulatory pressure
- competitive displacement
- macro irrelevance

--------------------------------------------------

C. EVOLUTIONARY COMPETITION ANALYSIS

Analyze whether markets are experiencing:

- leadership rotation
- dominant narrative transition
- technological replacement
- capital migration
- institutional preference shifts
- structural power transfer
- generational participation shifts

--------------------------------------------------

D. SURVIVAL STABILITY CLASSIFICATION

Classify structures as:

HIGH SURVIVABILITY
MODERATE SURVIVABILITY
FRAGILE SURVIVABILITY
DECLINING STRUCTURE
EXTINCTION RISK

Extinction-risk environments increase long-term instability.

--------------------------------------------------

E. LONG-TERM DOMINANCE ANALYSIS

Determine whether systems may become:

- dominant
- structurally important
- speculative but fragile
- temporary trends
- declining legacy structures
- emerging leadership systems

--------------------------------------------------

F. SURVIVAL PROBABILITY ENGINE

Estimate probability that current structures may lead to:

- long-term dominance
- sustained capital attraction
- structural decline
- narrative persistence
- obsolescence
- replacement
- fragmentation
- extinction risk

Use probability language only.

--------------------------------------------------

G. EXECUTION IMPLICATIONS

If survivability is strengthening:

- long-term continuation reliability may improve
- structural capital inflow probability may improve
- narrative persistence may improve

If extinction risk increases:

- long-term fragility increases
- capital flight risk increases
- volatility instability increases
- avoid excessive long-term conviction
