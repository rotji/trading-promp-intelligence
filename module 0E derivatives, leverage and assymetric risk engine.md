STEP 0E1 — PRODUCT STRUCTURE CLASSIFICATION

Identify whether instrument is:

- spot
- CFD
- futures
- perpetual swap
- option
- structured product
- leveraged ETF
- synthetic derivative

Different products create different risk structures.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0E2 — LEVERAGE FRAGILITY ENGINE

Analyze:

- liquidation risk
- margin pressure
- leverage compression
- forced closing probability
- volatility sensitivity
- funding pressure

High leverage increases fragility.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0E3 — LIQUIDATION CASCADE ENGINE

Detect conditions where:

- stop cascades
- margin calls
- forced liquidations
- volatility spikes
- thin liquidity
- leverage overcrowding

can produce explosive moves.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0E4 — ASYMMETRIC RISK ENGINE

Identify opportunities where:

- downside is capped
- upside remains open
- convex payoff exists
- small risk controls large exposure

Examples:
- options
- long volatility trades
- asymmetric macro positions

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0E5 — OPTIONS & VOLATILITY ENGINE

Analyze:

- implied volatility
- volatility expansion
- premium cost
- time decay
- directional exposure
- convexity potential
- hedging flows

Options are probability and volatility instruments, not merely directional bets.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0E6 — PROFESSIONAL RISK STRUCTURE ENGINE

Compare:

A. Linear leveraged exposure
vs
B. Fixed-risk asymmetric exposure

Determine which structure better suits current environment.

--------------------------------------------------

IMPORTANT RULES

1. High leverage creates hidden fragility.

2. Liquidation mechanics amplify volatility.

3. Options cap downside risk to premium paid.

4. Asymmetric payoff structures can outperform linear exposure.

5. Different derivatives behave differently during volatility shocks.

6. Fragility can create explosive opportunity.

7. Convexity is one of the most powerful forces in markets.

--------------------------------------------------
