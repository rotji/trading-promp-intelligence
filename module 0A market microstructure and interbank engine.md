--------------------------------------------------

MODULE 0A — MARKET MICROSTRUCTURE & INTERBANK ENGINE

Purpose:
Understand the structural, mechanical, institutional, and liquidity foundations underlying price movement across forex, stocks, commodities, crypto, and derivatives markets.

Markets are not merely charts.

Markets are networks of liquidity, participants, infrastructure, and transactional flows.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0A1 — MARKET STRUCTURE CLASSIFICATION

Identify the underlying structure of the market being traded.

Possible structures include:

- interbank market
- centralized exchange
- dealer market
- auction market
- OTC market
- derivative market
- CFD market
- synthetic broker market

Different structures produce different liquidity and execution behavior.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0A2 — PARTICIPANT HIERARCHY ENGINE

Identify major participant categories such as:

- central banks
- tier-1 banks
- market makers
- liquidity providers
- hedge funds
- institutions
- corporations
- sovereign funds
- retail brokers
- retail traders
- algorithmic systems
- passive flows

Different participants have different incentives and behavior.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0A3 — FLOW PURPOSE CLASSIFICATION

Determine whether dominant flows are likely driven by:

- hedging
- international trade
- funding needs
- liquidity management
- speculation
- portfolio rebalancing
- reserve management
- risk reduction
- derivatives hedging
- carry trade activity

Not all market activity is speculative.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0A4 — LIQUIDITY CONSUMPTION ENGINE

Analyze how price movement occurs through:

- liquidity consumption
- order flow imbalance
- depth exhaustion
- spread expansion
- market maker repricing
- liquidity withdrawal
- volatility shocks

Price moves when liquidity at current levels is consumed.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0A5 — MARKET MAKER BEHAVIOR ENGINE

Analyze market maker incentives such as:

- spread capture
- inventory management
- volatility avoidance
- liquidity provision
- hedging exposure
- quote adjustment
- risk reduction

Market makers often prefer stable, liquid environments.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0A6 — INTERMARKET EQUILIBRIUM ENGINE

Analyze mathematical and structural relationships between related markets such as:

- triangular currency relationships
- cross-asset pricing relationships
- interest rate differentials
- funding relationships
- arbitrage relationships
- derivative pricing relationships

Markets are interconnected systems.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0A7 — EXECUTION INFRASTRUCTURE ENGINE

Analyze execution environment quality including:

- broker infrastructure
- server stability
- feed reliability
- spread conditions
- session liquidity
- slippage risk
- execution latency
- after-hours conditions
- rollover instability

Execution infrastructure affects trading outcomes.

--------------------------------------------------

STEP 0A8 — MARKET ENVIRONMENT CLASSIFICATION

Classify current environment as:

- active institutional session
- premarket
- after-hours
- rollover
- low liquidity
- high liquidity
- news-driven
- algorithmically unstable
- volatility expansion
- compression environment

Market behavior changes with time and participation conditions.

--------------------------------------------------

IMPORTANT RULES

1. Markets are liquidity systems, not merely chart patterns.

2. Different participants operate with different incentives.

3. Most market flows are not purely speculative.

4. Price movement depends heavily on liquidity consumption and order flow imbalance.

5. Execution infrastructure affects real trading outcomes.

6. Market behavior changes dramatically across sessions and liquidity environments.

7. Intermarket relationships create structural dependencies between assets.
