MODULE 0 BRIDGE A

GLOBAL FUNDING ENGINE

PURPOSE

Monitor the global funding environment that powers:

* banks
* hedge funds
* sovereign funds
* corporations
* dealers
* derivatives markets

Detect whether funding conditions are:

* expanding
* neutral
* tightening
* stressed
* crisis-level

The objective is to identify hidden liquidity conditions before they fully appear in asset prices.

---

CORE PRINCIPLE

Modern markets are funding systems.

Asset prices are downstream.

Funding conditions are upstream.

When funding expands:

* leverage expands
* risk appetite expands
* volatility often compresses
* asset prices rise

When funding contracts:

* leverage contracts
* forced selling increases
* volatility expands
* liquidity deteriorates
* asset prices weaken

Funding conditions frequently move before markets react.

---

PRIMARY QUESTIONS

The engine must continuously evaluate:

1. Is global funding expanding?

2. Is global funding contracting?

3. Is dollar funding abundant?

4. Is dollar funding scarce?

5. Are banks comfortable extending balance sheets?

6. Are dealers reducing risk?

7. Is leverage increasing?

8. Is leverage decreasing?

9. Is liquidity creation accelerating?

10. Is liquidity destruction accelerating?

---

FUNDING REGIMES

REGIME 1

EXPANSIVE FUNDING

Characteristics:

* abundant liquidity
* low funding stress
* healthy leverage growth
* easy credit conditions
* strong risk appetite

Asset impact:

* bullish equities
* bullish crypto
* bullish carry trades
* tighter credit spreads

---

REGIME 2

NEUTRAL FUNDING

Characteristics:

* stable liquidity
* balanced risk taking
* moderate leverage

Asset impact:

* range conditions
* selective trends
* normal volatility

---

REGIME 3

TIGHTENING FUNDING

Characteristics:

* rising funding costs
* declining leverage
* increasing caution

Asset impact:

* weaker risk assets
* stronger defensive assets
* higher volatility

---

REGIME 4

STRESSED FUNDING

Characteristics:

* funding scarcity
* collateral stress
* dealer balance-sheet pressure
* liquidity withdrawal

Asset impact:

* violent volatility
* deleveraging
* liquidation cascades

---

REGIME 5

CRISIS FUNDING

Characteristics:

* systemic funding disruption
* central bank intervention
* emergency liquidity programs
* market dysfunction

Asset impact:

* crisis behavior
* disorderly repricing
* extreme volatility

---

GLOBAL FUNDING INPUTS

Monitor:

* BIS funding reports

* Fed liquidity operations
* ECB liquidity operations
* BOJ liquidity operations
* swap line activity
* repo conditions
* money market conditions
* commercial paper markets
* bank funding indicators

---

INTERPRETATION RULES

If funding expands:

Increase probability of:

* trend continuation
* risk-on behavior
* carry trades
* leverage expansion

---

If funding contracts:

Increase probability of:

* volatility expansion
* defensive positioning
* deleveraging
* risk-off behavior

---

EXECUTION RULE

Funding regime must influence:

* position size
* conviction level
* holding period
* portfolio aggressiveness

Aggressive positioning should only occur when funding conditions support risk taking.

---

FINAL PRINCIPLE

Funding is the fuel.

Liquidity is the transmission system.

Positioning is the accelerator.

Price is the result.
