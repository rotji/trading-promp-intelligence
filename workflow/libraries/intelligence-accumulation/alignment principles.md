# 1. Definition

## What Is A Carry Trade?

A carry trade is an investment strategy in which capital is borrowed at a relatively low funding cost and invested into assets that are expected to generate a higher return.

The return may come from one or more of the following sources:

• Interest rate differentials

• Bond yields

• Dividend income

• Capital appreciation

• Commodity appreciation

• Equity market appreciation

• Credit spreads

The objective is to earn a positive return that exceeds the total cost of funding while managing the risks associated with exchange-rate movements, asset-price fluctuations, and changes in global financial conditions.

---

## Fundamental Principle

A carry trade is not simply borrowing money cheaply.

It is the process of transferring capital from an environment where money is inexpensive to an environment where capital is expected to produce a superior risk-adjusted return.

This movement of capital is one of the primary mechanisms through which global liquidity is transmitted across financial markets.

## Carry Trade Is A Capital Flow Mechanism

Within GCFIOS, a carry trade is not viewed merely as a trading strategy.

It is viewed as a global capital-flow transmission mechanism.

Every carry trade represents capital leaving one funding environment and entering another investment environment.

As a result, carry trades influence:

• Foreign exchange markets

• Government bond markets

• Equity markets

• Credit markets

• Commodity markets

• Cryptocurrency markets

Because large institutional investors frequently employ carry strategies, changes in carry-trade activity can materially influence global liquidity conditions and cross-asset price behaviour.

---

## Core Objective

The primary objective of a carry trade is to maximise the spread between the cost of funding and the expected return on invested capital while maintaining an acceptable level of risk.

Successful carry trades depend not only on interest-rate differentials but also on stable funding conditions, supportive monetary policy, manageable volatility, and favourable market sentiment.

---

## GCFIOS Principle

Within GCFIOS, carry trades are classified as one of the primary global liquidity transmission mechanisms.

Changes in carry-trade activity are therefore analysed not only for their effect on currencies but also for their ability to alter liquidity, capital allocation, risk appetite, and cross-asset market behaviour.
