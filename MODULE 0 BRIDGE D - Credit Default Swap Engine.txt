MODULE 0 BRIDGE D

CREDIT DEFAULT SWAP ENGINE

PURPOSE

Monitor global credit risk conditions through CDS markets.

Detect:

* sovereign stress
* banking stress
* corporate credit deterioration
* systemic instability
* default fear

before those risks become fully visible in price.

---

CORE PRINCIPLE

Credit markets often detect danger before:

* equity markets
* FX markets
* commodity markets

When CDS spreads widen:

The market is demanding more compensation for risk.

This often signals:

* deteriorating confidence
* funding stress
* liquidity withdrawal
* rising crisis probability

---

WHAT IS A CDS

Credit Default Swap:

A contract providing protection against default.

Buyer:

Pays premium.

Seller:

Provides protection if default occurs.

CDS functions like insurance.

---

PRIMARY QUESTIONS

1. Is sovereign credit risk increasing?

2. Is sovereign credit risk decreasing?

3. Is banking system risk increasing?

4. Is banking system risk decreasing?

5. Is corporate credit deteriorating?

6. Is corporate credit improving?

7. Is systemic risk building?

8. Is systemic risk falling?

9. Are investors demanding more protection?

10. Are investors reducing protection demand?

---

CDS REGIMES

REGIME 1

CREDIT IMPROVEMENT

Characteristics:

* falling CDS spreads
* improving confidence
* strong credit conditions

Implications:

* bullish risk assets
* lower volatility
* healthy liquidity

---

REGIME 2

NEUTRAL CREDIT

Characteristics:

* stable spreads
* balanced risk perception

Implications:

* normal market behavior

---

REGIME 3

CREDIT DETERIORATION

Characteristics:

* widening CDS spreads
* increasing caution

Implications:

* defensive positioning
* reduced risk appetite

---

REGIME 4

CREDIT STRESS

Characteristics:

* rapidly widening spreads
* rising fear

Implications:

* volatility expansion
* liquidity contraction
* risk-off behavior

---

REGIME 5

SYSTEMIC CREDIT CRISIS

Characteristics:

* explosive CDS widening
* default concerns
* institutional instability

Implications:

* crisis regime
* deleveraging
* capital flight

MARKET IMPACT

Widening CDS spreads:

Increase probability of:

* defensive positioning
* stronger safe havens
* lower risk appetite
* liquidity contraction

---

Falling CDS spreads:

Increase probability of:

* risk-on behavior
* stronger growth assets
* expanding liquidity

---

EXECUTION RULE

Credit conditions must influence:

* portfolio aggressiveness
* position sizing
* volatility expectations
* crisis probability

---

WARNING SIGNALS

Raise risk warnings if:

* CDS diverges from equities
* CDS diverges from credit markets
* CDS widens while risk assets rise

These divergences frequently appear before major market repricing.

---

FINAL PRINCIPLE

Price can ignore risk temporarily.

Credit markets often cannot.

CDS markets are among the earliest warning systems for systemic instability.
