MODULE 8.3
PRECISION EXPANSION ENGINE
==================================================

PURPOSE
==================================================

The purpose of this module is determining:

* where continuation expansion remains favorable
* where asymmetry remains attractive
* where continuation persistence weakens
* where profit realization becomes justified
* where volatility expansion threatens continuation
* where liquidity deterioration threatens persistence
* where continuation durability supports extended participation

before trade management adaptation begins.

The objective is NOT arbitrary take-profit placement.

The objective is probabilistic expansion harvesting.

EXPANSION CLASSIFICATION ENGINE
==================================================

The system must classify expansion structure into:

1. EARLY EXPANSION
2. BALANCED EXPANSION
3. EXTENDED EXPANSION
4. NONLINEAR EXPANSION
5. EXHAUSTION EXPANSION

before realization assumptions occur.

==================================================
1. EARLY EXPANSION
==================================================

Characteristics:

* continuation still developing
* asymmetry still highly favorable
* volatility behavior controlled
* liquidity continuation constructive

Implications:

* continuation participation favored
* premature realization discouraged

Risk Characteristics:

* continuation confirmation still evolving

==================================================

* expansion quality constructive
* asymmetry still favorable
* volatility behavior manageable

* partial realization possible

3. EXTENDED EXPANSION
==================================================

* asymmetry compressing gradually
* volatility sensitivity increasing

==================================================

Continuation durability improves when:

* displacement quality remains constructive
* continuation persistence remains stable
* volatility behavior remains controlled
* liquidity continuation remains supportive
* higher timeframe inheritance remains aligned

* expansion becomes vertical and unstable
* volatility acceleration destabilizes
* liquidity continuation weakens
* exhaustion behavior emerges
* continuation persistence weakens materially

2. STRONG EXPANSION

4. FRAGILE EXPANSION
