MODULE 0 BRIDGE B

FX SWAP ENGINE

PURPOSE

Monitor the global FX swap market to identify:

* dollar funding stress
* dollar abundance
* carry trade stability
* cross-currency funding pressure
* hidden liquidity shortages

Detect funding conditions before they fully appear in:

* FX markets
* bond markets
* equity markets
* commodity markets

---

CORE PRINCIPLE

The world runs on dollars.

Most global institutions require dollar funding.

FX swaps are one of the primary mechanisms used to obtain dollars.

Therefore:

FX swap markets are a real-time health monitor of global liquidity.

---

WHAT IS AN FX SWAP

Basic structure:

Currency A exchanged for Currency B today.

Transaction reversed later.

Example:

Japanese bank:

* provides JPY
* receives USD

Later:

* returns USD
* receives JPY back

Purpose:

Temporary funding.

Not speculation.

---

PRIMARY QUESTIONS

1. Is dollar demand rising?

2. Is dollar demand falling?

3. Is offshore dollar funding becoming scarce?

4. Is offshore dollar funding becoming abundant?

5. Are funding spreads widening?

6. Are funding spreads tightening?

7. Is carry becoming attractive?

8. Is carry becoming dangerous?

9. Is FX liquidity improving?

10. Is FX liquidity deteriorating?

---

FX SWAP REGIMES

REGIME 1

DOLLAR ABUNDANCE

Characteristics:

* low funding stress
* stable carry
* healthy liquidity

Implications:

* bullish risk assets
* stable FX
* lower volatility

---

REGIME 2

NEUTRAL FUNDING

Characteristics:

* balanced liquidity
* stable funding markets

Implications:

* normal market behavior

---

REGIME 3

DOLLAR TIGHTENING

Characteristics:

* rising dollar demand
* widening funding spreads

Implications:

* stronger USD
* weaker carry trades
* higher volatility

---

REGIME 4

DOLLAR STRESS

Characteristics:

* funding shortages
* aggressive dollar demand

Implications:

* risk-off behavior
* funding pressure
* emerging market weakness

---

REGIME 5

DOLLAR CRISIS

Characteristics:

* severe funding shortage
* central bank intervention

Implications:

* emergency liquidity operations
* swap line activation
* systemic instability

---

KEY INPUTS

Monitor:

* BIS FX swap statistics
* cross-currency basis swaps
* dollar funding spreads
* Fed swap line activity
* offshore dollar funding markets
* major bank funding conditions

---

CARRY TRADE ASSESSMENT

Evaluate:

* USDJPY carry conditions
* AUDJPY carry conditions
* NZDJPY carry conditions
* emerging market carry conditions

Determine:

* stable carry
* vulnerable carry
* carry unwind probability

---

MARKET IMPACT

If dollar funding improves:

Increase probability of:

* risk-on behavior
* carry continuation
* lower volatility

---

If dollar funding deteriorates:

Increase probability of:

* risk-off behavior
* carry unwind
* volatility expansion
* defensive positioning

---

EXECUTION RULE

Carry trades should never be evaluated solely through price.

Always evaluate:

* funding conditions
* dollar demand
* swap conditions
* liquidity conditions

before executing.

---

FINAL PRINCIPLE

FX prices move because of many forces.

FX funding conditions often move before FX prices.

The FX swap market is one of the hidden engines of global liquidity.
