- highest asymmetry
- lowest fill probability
- may never trigger

- market is not running aggressively
- session has enough time remaining

- patience is required

--------------------------------------------------

B. ACCESSIBLE ELITE ENTRY

This is the balanced execution entry.

Characteristics:

- good reward-to-risk
- realistic fill probability
- enters nearer to current market structure
- less perfect than extreme entry
- more executable than sniper entry

Use when:

- trend is already strong
- retracements are shallow
- buyers/sellers are defending price early
- market refuses deep discount
- momentum remains active

--------------------------------------------------

C. CONFIRMATION ENTRY

This is the breakout/validation entry.

Characteristics:

- lower reward-to-risk
- higher confirmation
- highest probability of participation
- usually entered after breakout, reclaim, or continuation confirmation

Use when:

- price refuses to retrace
- breakout is clean
- market structure confirms direction
- momentum expansion is active
- missing the move is more likely than deep retracement

For each trade idea, the system must choose:

- 1 primary entry model
- optional 1 backup entry model
- maximum 2 active entry plans

Multiple entries do NOT mean multiple full-risk trades.

Module 5 positions are not static.

After trade activation, position management becomes adaptive based on market behavior.

The system continuously evaluates:

- momentum strength
- liquidity behavior
- volatility expansion/contraction
- candle aggression
- session timing
- structure continuation or weakening

This allows dynamic adjustment of:

- stop losses
- take profits
- exposure size
- scaling structure
- runner positions

---

## A. Breakeven Protection Logic

IF:
price reaches TP1
OR
price moves strongly in favor

THEN:
move stop loss to breakeven (entry price).

Purpose:
eliminate risk on remaining position.

---

## B. Dynamic Stop Loss Logic

IF:
market structure continues favorably

THEN:
stop loss may trail upward/downward behind:
- higher lows
- lower highs
- liquidity zones
- structure pivots

Purpose:
lock in increasing profit while allowing trend continuation.

---

## C. Dynamic Take Profit Logic

Take profits are flexible — not permanently fixed.

IF:
momentum accelerates strongly
AND
higher timeframe structure remains intact

THEN:
TP levels may be extended.

IF:
momentum weakens
OR
reversal signals appear

THEN:
TP levels may be reduced to secure profits earlier.

---

## D. Partial Exit Logic

Positions may be reduced gradually as price moves toward targets.

Example:
- partial close at TP1
- additional close at TP2
- runner position left open for extended continuation

Purpose:
balance profit security with larger opportunity capture.

---

## E. Trailing Management Logic

IF:
trend becomes strong continuation trend

THEN:
SL may continuously trail behind structure.

This creates:
dynamic profit locking.

---

## F. Volatility Adaptation Logic

IF:
market volatility increases aggressively

THEN:
- wider trailing behavior may be used
- TP expansion allowed
- early exits avoided

IF:
volatility contracts
OR
market becomes choppy

THEN:
- profits secured faster
- tighter management applied

---

## G. Session-Based Management

Management behavior changes by session:

- London open
- NY open
- overlap sessions
- low liquidity sessions

High liquidity sessions allow:
- larger continuation targets
- aggressive runners

Low liquidity sessions require:
- tighter protection
- faster exits

---

## H. Alert-Based Manual Management

Price alerts may be placed at:
- TP1 zones
- liquidity zones
- structure pivots
- volatility thresholds

When alerts trigger:
manual reassessment and adjustment occur.

This creates semi-automated professional management.

STEP 28B — SESSION DECAY MANAGEMENT

As major liquidity sessions close,
the system must reassess trade quality.

Late-session environments often produce:

- weaker continuation
- random oscillation
- lower institutional participation
- increased noise relative to directional movement

Rules:

If after London close and late NY session:

- momentum weakens
- structure stalls
- volatility compresses
- no catalyst exists
- price remains near entry

then partial close or full close becomes allowed.

However:

Do NOT close automatically if:

- strong trend expansion exists
- macro catalyst remains active
- higher timeframe continuation structure remains strong
- trade is already deeply in profit with trailing protection

The system must classify:

1. Expanding Trend Environment
2. Decaying Session Environment
3. Transition Compression Environment

Only Transition Compression and Decaying Session environments justify time-based exits.

STEP 29 — FINAL EXECUTION CONFIRMATION
GAP / REPRICING EXECUTION FILTER

Before approving execution, check whether current price action is occurring after a major gap or sudden repricing event.

If gap instability exists:

- reduce confidence
- avoid chasing first move
- wait for confirmation
- use wider invalidation logic
- reduce position size
- mark setup as WATCHLIST unless structure stabilizes

If gap is structural and continuation is confirmed:

execution may proceed only after price accepts the new value area.
Classify setup as:

PENDING ORDER READY
ACTIVE EXECUTION
AVOID
INVALIDATED

Definitions:

PENDING ORDER READY:
Module 5 found a valid zone, order type, stop loss, and target.

ACTIVE EXECUTION:
price already triggered the planned pending order zone.

AVOID:
no clean setup or risk/reward is poor.

INVALIDATED:
market story failed before execution.

Rules:

Do NOT say ENTER NOW unless price is already inside the planned refined entry zone and risk/reward is still valid.

Do NOT chase strong candles.

If price has already moved far away from the best entry zone:
→ use PENDING ORDER READY or AVOID, not market entry.
